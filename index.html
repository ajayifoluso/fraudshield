What does FraudShield AI actually do?
FraudShield AI is an AI-powered fraud detection and AML screening platform that works across all payment methods and fund transfer types — ACH, wire transfers, SWIFT, SEPA, crypto, P2P, mobile money, and card payments. A compliance analyst enters details about a transaction including payer identity, payee identity, amount, and transfer type. The system returns a risk score, explainable signals, and cross-checks against 8 external databases. The goal is to flag suspicious activity before it causes damage and generate a full regulatory paper trail.
Does it only detect card fraud?
No — card fraud is just one of many scenarios it covers. FraudShield detects fraud and suspicious activity across all payment types. A suspicious wire transfer to a sanctioned country, a structuring pattern in ACH payments, a crypto transfer to a flagged wallet, a P2P payment to a first-time recipient at 3am — all of these are analyzed with the same risk engine, OFAC/FinCEN screening, network graph analysis, and AML typology matching. Card-specific signals like BIN lookup and CVV verification only activate when a card type is selected.
How does it detect fraud without a card number?
For non-card transactions (wires, ACH, crypto, P2P), fraud is detected through behavioral and contextual signals: the relationship between payer and payee, transaction amount vs. historical baseline, destination country risk, time of day, IP/country mismatch, structuring patterns, payer KYC status, payee sanctions screening, and hidden network connections between accounts. These signals are weighted by 5 AI models to produce the final risk score — no card number needed.
Which databases does it check?
Every analysis — regardless of payment type — runs checks against 8 data sources: OFAC SDN (US Treasury sanctions), FinCEN 314(a) (law enforcement subjects), Interpol (Red Notices & global watchlists), Device Fingerprint (fraud device database), Network Graph DB (hidden account connections), AML Typologies (money laundering patterns), BIN Database (card transactions only), and Card Hotlist (card transactions only). In this proof of concept, responses are simulated. In production, each fires a live API call within 50–350ms.
What is the bigger goal of this platform?
FraudShield AI is designed to help financial institutions catch fraud, money laundering, and suspicious activity before it causes damage — across every payment method and fund transfer channel — while simultaneously generating a regulatory paper trail presentable to FinCEN, the US Treasury, and USCIS. Every analysis produces a timestamped case ID, an exportable audit log, and an AI-assisted SAR draft aligned to BSA / 31 CFR 1010 and FATF Recommendations 16 & 20.
Is this a finished product?
This is a proof of concept demonstrating the system's architecture, data flows, AI models, and regulatory alignment. Database checks are simulated with realistic placeholder responses. In a production deployment, each check would fire a live API call to the listed data source (OFAC API, FinCEN Secure Portal, Rippleshot, Sardine, etc.). The scoring models, signal logic, SAR generation, and audit trail are fully functional in this version.
