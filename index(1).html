About FraudShield AI

FraudShield AI is an enterprise-grade fraud detection intelligence platform built to combat the growing threat of GenAI-enabled financial crime in the United States banking system.

The system implements a four-model machine learning ensemble — Isolation Forest, Random Forest, Logistic Regression, and a rule-based threshold engine — with per-model explainability scoring and real-time mapping against the six fraud typologies documented in FinCEN's November 2024 national alert on generative AI deepfake fraud.

Developed as part of an active research and development endeavor focused on protecting U.S. financial system integrity through AI-driven compliance tooling.

Built and maintained by Foluso Ajayi · Cyfendry · Dallas, TX · 2026

How the AI Works

Isolation Forest — detects anomalous transactions by isolating outliers from normal behavioral patterns using unsupervised learning.

Random Forest — classifies risk using an ensemble of decision trees trained on known fraud indicators including KYC status and jurisdiction.

Logistic Regression — produces probability-weighted fraud scores based on statistically significant feature combinations.

Threshold Rules Engine — applies hard BSA/AML compliance rules including structuring detection and sanctions screening.
